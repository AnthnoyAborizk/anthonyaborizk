Anthony Aborizk

About Me

Anthony Aborizk is presently a Ph.D. candidate in Aerospace Engineering at the University of Florida (UF) under the advisory of Dr. Norman Fitz-Coy. He received a B.S. in mechanical engineering while researching orbital debris characterization, a M.S. in aerospace engineering with a focus in Dynamic Systems and Controls, and a graduate certificate in machine learning during his time at UF.

Anthony's research focus presently centers around the use of reinforcement learning strategies in tandem with classical controller algorithms to support on-board spacecraft guidance. He is exploring spacecraft autonomy via simulation of rendezvous, proximity operations, and docking. He has published his Ph.D. proposal to the 2022 AAS/AIAA Astrodynamics Specialist Conference in Charlotte, NC.

During his undergraduate experience, he conducted research on the DebriSat hypervelocity breakup experiment at UF which extended to an internship with the NASA Johnson Space Center. He sought to explain the underlying physics of projectile ballistics via the development of image processing algorithms to extract relevant optical properties of satellite fragments from foam panels. He helped unveil new insights on shape and size statistics of orbital debris items. Additionally, he developed a neural network for anomaly detection of gas turbine engines during an internship with Power Systems Mfg.
